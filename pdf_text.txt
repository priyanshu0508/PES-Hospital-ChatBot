1. What is this document? 

This document explains what our chatbot does, why we are building it, how it works, and how we plan 
to build it. We have written it in simple language so anyone — whether technical or not — can read and 
understand it. 
This is a student internship project done for PES University Hospital, Bengaluru. 
 
2. What is the problem we are solving? 

PES Hospital gets around 1,800 to 2,000 patients every single day at the OPD (Out-Patient Department). 
Every one of them has to go to the registration counter first before seeing a doctor. 
 
Here is what happens at the counter right now:- 
 
Step 1. Patient walks in and stands in a queue at the counter. 
Step 2. Counter staff asks for their Aadhaar card (compulsory) and collects details — name, 
age, address, complaint. 

Step 3. Staff manually types everything into the hospital system. 
Step 4. Based on what the patient says, staff decides which OPD department to send them 
to. 

Step 5. Patient gets a token slip and goes to that OPD floor. 
 
This process has three real problems:- 
 
Problem What actually happens because of it 
Long queues Patients wait 20–40 minutes just to register, especially in morning 
peak hours between 9 AM and 11 AM 
Language barrier Patients speak Kannada, Telugu, Tamil, Hindi and more. Counter staff 
cannot always understand everyone, leading to wrong department 
allocation 
Manual data entry Typing everything by hand is slow and sometimes creates duplicate 
patient records in the system 
 
3. What is our solution? 

We are building a chatbot — basically a smart registration form that talks to the patient in their own 
language. The patient can open it on their phone by scanning a QR code at the hospital entrance, or use 
a tablet that the hospital keeps at the entrance (called a kiosk). 
 
The chatbot does the registration job before the patient reaches the counter. The counter still exists 
and still works — it just gets far fewer people standing in front of it. 
 
No app download needed. It opens directly in the phone's browser, just like a website.

4. How does the chatbot work? — Step by step 

Here is exactly what happens when a patient uses our chatbot:- 
 
1. Patient arrives at the hospital entrance and either scans the QR code on their phone or walks up 
to the kiosk tablet.

2. The chatbot opens — no login, no account, no password needed. 

3. Patient picks their language — Kannada, Hindi, English, Telugu, or Tamil. The entire chatbot 
switches to that language instantly. 

4. Chatbot asks:- are you a new patient or have you visited before? 
5. If new patient — chatbot collects: name, age, gender, Aadhaar number, and mobile number. A 
unique Patient ID (called UHID) is automatically created and saved. 

6. If returning patient — they type their existing UHID. Their details are already saved, so they skip 
the full form. 

7. Patient taps big symptom buttons to describe what is wrong — for example:- Fever, Eye problem, 
Chest pain, Stomach pain, Bone/Joint pain, Skin problem, Child health, Ear/Nose/Throat. 

8. Chatbot looks at the selected symptoms and tells the patient which OPD department and floor to 
go to. For example: Fever → General Medicine, Floor 1, Room 101. 

9. A token slip appears on the patient's phone screen showing their token number, department, 
floor, and time. They show this screen to the staff at the OPD entrance — exactly like showing a 
paper slip today.

5. What exactly does the chatbot do? 

5.1 For the patient:- 

• Greets them in their own language 
• Asks simple questions one at a time — like a conversation, not a big form 
• Reads questions out loud using voice so even low-literacy patients can follow 
• Accepts voice input — patient can speak their complaint instead of typing 
• Shows big tap-friendly buttons for symptoms — no spelling, no typing 
• Tells them exactly which floor and room to go to 
• Shows a clean token slip on screen with all their details 
 
5.2 For the counter staff:- 

• A simple dashboard page shows all patients who registered through the chatbot today 
• Staff can see each patient's name, token number, department, and time of registration 
• Staff can still manually register patients who walked directly to the counter 
• Both chatbot patients and counter patients appear in the same queue view 
 
5.3 For the hospital admin:- 

• A graphs page shows useful daily data — how many patients per department, what time the rush 
happens, which languages patients use most 
• This is data the hospital currently does not have — our system generates it automatically as a 
side effect of digital registration 
 
5.4 How does it decide which department to send the patient to? 

We maintain a simple list that maps symptoms to departments. When the patient taps their symptoms, 
the system checks this list and gives the matching department. Examples: 
 
Patient taps these symptoms Goes to this department Floor 
Fever, cold, cough, body ache, weakness General Medicine Floor 1 
Chest pain, heart racing, breathlessness Cardiology Floor 2 
Eye pain, blurry vision, redness Ophthalmology Floor 2 
Stomach pain, vomiting, diarrhoea, acidity Gastroenterology Floor 1 
Bone pain, joint pain, back pain Orthopaedics Floor 3 
Skin rash, itching, hair loss Dermatology Floor 3 
Child fever, vaccination, child growth Paediatrics Floor 4 
Toothache, gum pain, cavity Dental Floor 1 
Ear pain, hearing problem, throat pain ENT Floor 2 
Anything not matched above General Medicine (default) Floor 1

6. What we are NOT doing? 

To keep the project realistic for an 8-week internship, we have clearly decided NOT to build the 
following: 
 
Not in our project Why we left it out 
Doctor appointment scheduling That is a separate hospital system — too complex 
for this scope 
OPD floor management after patient arrives Once the patient has their token and goes to the 
floor, our job is done 
Payment or billing Completely separate system, not related to 
registration 
Medical diagnosis We only suggest a department — we are not 
doctors 
Emergency handling People in real emergencies run straight to the 
emergency ward — they do not stop to scan a QR 
code 

Integration with existing hospital software Their current system is separate — connecting to it 
is a much bigger project 
Mobile app (Android/iOS) Web app is enough — no download needed, works 
on any phone browser

7. What tools and technologies are we using? 

Our team has decided to use the MERN stack. Here is what each part does in simple terms: 
 
Name What it is What it does in our project 
MongoDB Database Stores all patient information, visit records, and token 
numbers. Free cloud version (Atlas) used. 
Express.js Backend framework Handles the logic on the server — saves patient data, 
generates UHID, generates token number, sends data 
back to the screen. 

React.js Frontend / UI Builds the chatbot screens the patient sees — language 
selection, symptom buttons, token slip screen, counter 
dashboard. 
Node.js Server Runs Express.js. Think of it as the engine that keeps the 
backend alive. 
i18next Translation library Handles switching all text to the patient's chosen 
language. We write translation files for each language 
once. 

Web Speech API Voice (built into browser) Lets the chatbot speak questions aloud and also accept 
voice input from the patient. Completely free — no API 
key needed. 
Recharts Graphs library for React Draws the bar charts and graphs on the admin 
dashboard. Free and simple to use. 
Vercel Hosting for frontend Puts our React app live on the internet. Free for 
students. 
Render Hosting for backend Puts our Node/Express server live on the internet. Free 
tier available. 
 
8. How is the data stored? 

We use two separate storage buckets in MongoDB — one for patient details and one for each visit. This 
way a patient's personal information is saved only once, even if they visit the hospital 10 times. 
 
Collection (bucket) What it stores 
patients Name, age, gender, Aadhaar number, mobile, address, UHID. Saved 
once when patient registers for the first time. 
visits Which department, which symptoms selected, token number, date 
and time, new or follow-up visit. A new entry is created every single 
visit. 
 
Token numbers are generated automatically. The system counts how many patients have already gone 
to a department today and adds 1. So the 47th person going to General Medicine today gets token GM-
047. It resets to GM-001 every morning.

9. How are we going to build this? 

We are building this in 5 stages over 8 weeks. The most important rule is: get the basic thing working 
first, then add extras on top. We do not try to build everything at once. 
 
Stage When What we build 

Stage 1 Week 1–2 React UI only — language selection screen, symptom picker buttons, 
token slip screen. No backend yet. Everything is hardcoded just to see 
how it looks and flows. 

Stage 2 Week 3 Backend and database — Node + Express server, MongoDB connected, 
patient data saved, UHID auto-generated, token auto-generated, sent 
back to the screen. 

Stage 3 Week 4 Connect everything — React calls the real backend. Full flow works end 
to end. Patient registers → data saved → token shown. This is the first 
real working demo. 

Stage 4 Week 5–6 Voice and languages — Web Speech API added for voice input and 
output. i18next added for language switching. More symptom buttons 
added based on hospital feedback. 

Stage 5 Week 7–8 Dashboard and graphs — Counter staff queue view, daily patient count 
graphs, department-wise charts, peak hour chart. Testing with real 
hospital staff. Final demo. 
 
10. Who is doing what? 

Frontend Building the chatbot screens in React — what the patient sees and taps 
Backend Building the server in Node.js + Express — saving data, generating 
tokens, sending responses 
Database Setting up MongoDB Atlas, designing the patient and visit collections 
Voice and Languages Integrating Web Speech API for voice and i18next for multilingual 
support 

Dashboard and Graphs Building the counter staff view and admin analytics page using Recharts 
Testing Testing the full flow with real users at the hospital, fixing issues, 
preparing demo
